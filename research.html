Research

The research I have participated in so far has been far have included observational astronomy, using data from the University of Oregon's Pine Mountain Observatory in Bend, Oregon, as well as from the Gemini telescopes located in Hilo, Hawaii. I have worked on computational astrophysics research, where I use UofO's ACISS supercomputer to simulate Type Ia supernovae and Rossby Wave Instabilities. I have also been a part of genomics and biology research, where I have worked with the Brenden-Colson Center,Stand Up 2 Cancer pancreatic Cancer, Global Alliance For Genomics and Health, and OregonHealth and Science University labs.

Gemini/Hubble Space Telescope Galaxy Cluster Project

Gemini Observatory
University of Oregon Physics Department

I worked on analyzing spectrometric and photometric data from the Gemini Telescopes and Hubble Space Telescope as part of the Gemini/HST Galaxy Cluster Project looking at the evolution of field galaxies vs cluster galaxies. The poster describing the findings was presented at the International Astronomical Union meeting in Hawaii during August of 2015.

Type Ia Supernovae Simulation

University of Oregon Physics Department
February 2015 - Present

My research involves running simulations on the University of Oregon's ACISS High Performance Computing Cluster to simulate white dwarf stars from various initial conditions through supernovae or disintegration. The main goal is to determine what initial conditions lead to Type Ia supernovae. We are also simulating Rossby Wave Instabilities.

Stand Up 2 Cancer Pacnreatic Cancer Team
Oregon Health and Science University

The Aperio Consolidator takes the file output from Aperio imaging devices, combines them, and runs some summary statistics on the data files. The main purpose was to help with imaging pancreatic cancer cells in mice.
