Research

I have also been a part of genomics and biology research, where I have worked with the Brenden-Colson Center, Stand Up 2 Cancer pancreatic Cancer, Global Alliance For Genomics and Health, and Oregon Health and Science University labs.

Type Ia Supernovae Simulation

University of Oregon Physics Department
February 2015 - Present

My research involves running simulations on the University of Oregon's ACISS High Performance Computing Cluster to simulate white dwarf stars from various initial conditions through supernovae or disinigration. The main goal is to determine what initial conditions lead to Type Ia supernovae.
