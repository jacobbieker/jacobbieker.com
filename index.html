Interests

My interests are mostly in computing and physics, especially where the two intersect. Outside of software, I am really interested in alternative forms of input, such as the Myo, Leap Motion, and Oculus Rift; wearable technology, such as smartwatches and Google Glass or Microsoft HoloLens; robotics, especially quadcopters and drones, and computer hardware. I've also worked teaching elementary school kids how to operate and program Lego Robotics machines, play lacrosse, and like to hike and travel.

Experience

For the past three years, I have worked with Java and Android development. In this time, I have worked with wearable technology, such as the Myo armband, and with other input methods, such as the Leap Motion. As part of creating the Open Exoplanet Android App, I've become familiar with Python scripting, and SQLite databases. I have also worked with web technologies, such as HTML5, CSS, and Javascript, when modifying or creating websites for clients. I am just starting to get into scientific and parallel computing, such as MatLab and Fortran, through working with a professor on Type Ia supernova simulations on the University of Oregon's High Performance Computing cluster.
